Feelz on Crate-Digging & the Records That Built the Club

F
By the Thiiird Place team·May 2026·7 min read

Before Thiiird Place was a club, it was a crate. Feelz has been collecting and curating for years, and the philosophy that runs the whole community started in the same place every good night does — with a stack of records and a question: what does this room need to hear next?

We sat down with Feelz to talk about taste, the vinyl that started it all, and why curating for a room beats curating for an algorithm.

It starts with the dig

What got you into crate-digging in the first place?

"Honestly, it was the hunt. Streaming gives you everything instantly, but digging makes you earn it. You flip through a hundred records to find the one that changes a night. That patience teaches you taste — you stop chasing what's popular and start trusting what moves you."

"A record store will humble you. It teaches you that you don't know everything — and that's the fun part."

Is there a record that feels like the origin of Thiiird Place?

"There's no single one, but there's a feeling — that warm, late-Sunday, soulful pocket. Neo-soul, alternative R&B, the stuff that sounds like a conversation. The first time I cleared a room's nervous energy with a record like that, I knew that was the whole point. Make people feel safe enough to actually listen."

Curating for a room, not an algorithm

"An algorithm optimizes for the next click. A room is different. You're reading faces, energy, the time of night. You can take a risk because you can feel whether it landed. That feedback loop is everything, and you only get it when people are actually present."

That belief is why Thiiird Place is members-only and built around real listening sessions. The goal isn't reach — it's the room.

Advice for someone building their own taste?

"Listen wider than your comfort zone, then play it for people. Taste isn't private — it grows when you share it. That's literally what the club is for."
